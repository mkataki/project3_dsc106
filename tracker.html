Zone 1 (50-60%)

Zone 1 – Very Light (50-60% of MHR)
- Purpose: Recovery, warm-ups, and cool-downs
- Benefits: Improves overall circulation, aids muscle recovery, and helps build a base for endurance.
- Feeling: Easy effort, can carry a full conversation.

Zone 2 (60-70%)

Zone 2 – Light (60-70% of MHR)
- Purpose: Base-building, aerobic endurance
- Benefits: Improves fat metabolism, strengthens the heart, and enhances aerobic capacity.
- Feeling: Comfortable, steady effort; still able to speak in full sentences.

Zone 3 (70-80%)

Zone 3 – Moderate (70-80% of MHR)
- Purpose: Aerobic conditioning, steady-state training
- Benefits: Increases cardiovascular efficiency and builds endurance.
- Feeling: Breathing gets heavier, speaking in short sentences is possible.

Zone 4 (80-90%)

Zone 4 – Hard (80-90% of MHR)
- Purpose: Lactate threshold, improving speed and performance
- Benefits: Enhances the body's ability to sustain high-intensity efforts and delays fatigue.
- Feeling: Heavy breathing, difficult to talk, sustainable for short durations.

Zone 5 (90-100%)

Zone 5 – Maximum Effort (90-100% of MHR)
- Purpose: Peak performance, short bursts of maximal effort
- Benefits: Increases power, speed, and anaerobic capacity.
- Feeling: All-out effort, very hard to sustain, talking is nearly impossible.

Project Writeup

What have you done so far?

So far, we have developed a functional heart rate zone tracker that dynamically updates based on user input. The page includes interactive elements such as a speed slider, gender selection, and age-based heart rate prediction. Additionally, we have implemented an animated Mario or Peach character that runs across the screen, with animation speed adjusting smoothly to match the selected speed. We also created a structured layout where heart rate zones are visually displayed, and each zone's description is dynamically revealed when highlighted. The interface has been designed to be user-friendly, ensuring that all components are well-organized and intuitive to interact with. Also, we have ensured that the heart rate calculation logic follows a scientific approach, using age and speed to estimate maximum heart rate effectively. Overall, the project integrates both data-driven decision-making and interactive Uwe elements to make the experience engaging.

What will be the most challenging part of your project to design and why?

The most challenging part of the project will be ensuring smooth, real-time interactivity between all elements while maintaining a visually appealing layout. One major difficulty is keeping the animation smooth when adjusting speed, as reapplying animations can cause the character to restart instead of continuing naturally. Another challenge is ensuring that all UI elements scale properly across different screen sizes and devices, requiring responsive design techniques. Additionally, integrating real-time data visualization for heart rate tracking could be complex, as it would require dynamically updating charts based on user input. Ensuring accurate heart rate calculations that consider multiple factors, including gender differences and intensity levels, will also be critical. Lastly, refining the user experience by adding better feedback mechanisms, such as color transitions or visual cues when heart rate zones change, will require careful design and testing. Overcoming these challenges will involve optimizing JavaScript functions, refining CSS animations, and implementing efficient event handling strategies.
